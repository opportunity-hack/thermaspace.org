+WHAT IT DOES

Thermal imaging that lives in your room

Phone thermal cameras show you a tiny screen. ThermaSpace puts the heat where it actually is — on your walls, your equipment, your house — and remembers it between sessions.

🔒 Live lock

Lock any placed capture and it becomes a live instrument — every time you glance at it, it refreshes to what the camera sees right now. A thermal gauge for your breaker panel or printer bed.

📡 Export over your Wi-Fi

One ZIP with color PNGs, raw radiometric data, and context photos — served straight from the headset to your browser. Off by default, protected by a session code.

🛡 Nothing leaves the headset

No account, no cloud, no telemetry. Every capture and temperature history stays in private storage on your Quest. Read the whole privacy policy — it fits in a paragraph.

+SETUP

Working in about a minute

  1. Plug in the FLIR One

    The same USB-C camera you use with your phone goes straight into the Quest's USB-C port. Press its side button — the LED pulses when it's on.

  2. Seat it in the mount

    The 3D-printed holder sits the camera above your visor, aimed where you look. No mount yet? Hand-holding the camera works fine for a first try.

  3. Look around

    ThermaSpace connects automatically and a one-minute tour teaches the basics by doing: overlay on, first capture, gallery. Controllers and bare hands both work.

+WHY THIS EXISTS

Built to spark curiosity

ThermaSpace is a project of Opportunity Hack, a nonprofit hackathon community that writes code for good. It exists to further education and get people curious about what's around them — the heat you can suddenly see, and the code that makes it visible.

Every layer is open for students to pull apart: reverse-engineering a USB protocol, calibrating a radiometric sensor, mapping a room in 3D, anchoring content to real walls. If you teach, tinker, or mentor — this is for you, and we'd love to hear what you do with it.

+QUESTIONS

FAQ

Which FLIR One cameras work?

Any FLIR One with a USB-C connector: FLIR One Gen 3, FLIR One Pro LT, and FLIR One Pro. The Lightning (iOS) models don't fit the Quest's USB-C port and aren't supported.

Which headsets are supported?

Meta Quest 3 and Quest 3S. ThermaSpace relies on color passthrough and the USB-C port, so earlier Quests aren't supported.

Is this real thermal imaging or an effect?

Real. ThermaSpace streams the FLIR One's radiometric sensor over USB — every pixel is a real temperature in °C or °F, with a spot meter and min/max span readout. The "thermal vision" apps already on the store are simulations; this one requires the physical camera.

Do I need a PC or a phone?

No. The camera plugs straight into the headset and everything runs on the Quest. A browser on the same Wi-Fi can optionally view the live stream and download your captures.

Does it collect my data?

No. Nothing is collected or transmitted off the device — no account, no analytics sent anywhere. See the privacy policy.

Where do I get the app?

It's headed to the Meta Horizon Store. Until then, fill out the signup form to join the beta — you'll get a build and sideload instructions.

Can I use the FLIR One I already own?

Yes — that's the point. If it has USB-C, unplug it from your phone and plug it into your Quest. Nothing is modified; it keeps working with your phone afterward.
